Who am I?


My name is Choi Seongbin (but Cui Shengbin officially). I am a Ph.D. student at Osaka University studying cognitive neuroscience with Brain Physiology Lab and Center for Information and Neural Networks (CiNet).

Research


My research focuses on interoceptive processing and its role in moral decision-making and other social cognition. My work combines advanced neuroimaging techniques such as EEG and ECG to study the neural mechanisms underlying utilitarian and deontological decisions in moral dilemmas, as well as trustworthiness judgments of facial stimuli.

I also explore the intersection of computational modeling and neuroscience to uncover how human visual perception works.
