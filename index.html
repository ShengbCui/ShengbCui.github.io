Exploring the Symphony of the Mind

A Cognitive Neuroscience Researcher at the intersection of interoception, social decision-making, and music cognition.

Introduction

Welcome to my digital space. I am a cognitive neuroscience researcher fascinated by how our brains process complex auditory information, particularly music. My work aims to unravel the neural mechanisms underlying music perception and the profound emotional impact of music. I believe that by understanding how we perceive a symphony, we can better understand the architecture of the human mind itself.

Beyond the lab, I'm an avid musician and music enthusiast, which constantly fuels my scientific curiosity. This website is a fusion of these two worlds—a place to share my research, thoughts on science, and musical explorations.

Research Topics

Interoception

I investigate how heartbeat-evoked potentials (HEP) and related bodily signals are represented in cortex and how these interoceptive cues shape perception, attention, and valuation across tasks.

Social Decision-Making

I study neural mechanisms of moral choices and face-based trustworthiness judgments, testing how interoceptive processing (e.g., HEP) and large-scale networks such as precuneus/PCC and vmPFC contribute to evaluating others and guiding social behavior.

Visual Neuroscience

I use inverted encoding models to reconstruct orientation-selective population codes, examining how task demands and attention modulate the fidelity of visual feature representations.

Music & Emotion

I explore how rhythm, meter, and predictive timing influence perception and affect, linking temporal expectations and bodily states to the experience of musical structure.
