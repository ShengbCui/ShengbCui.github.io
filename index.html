About Me

My name is Choi Seongbin (but Cui Shengbin officially).

I am a Ph.D. student at The University of Osaka studying cognitive neuroscience with the Brain Physiology Lab and Center for Information and Neural Networks (CiNet).

Research Focus

Exploring the neural mechanisms of interoception and moral cognition

Interoceptive Processing

Investigating how internal bodily signals influence our cognitive processes and decision-making, particularly through EEG and ECG methodologies.

Moral Decision-Making

Examining the neural underpinnings of utilitarian and deontological decisions in moral dilemmas and their relationship to bodily signals.

Social Cognition

Studying how interoceptive awareness influences our social interactions and understanding of others' mental states.

Computational Modeling

Applying machine learning techniques to model and understand the mechanisms of human visual perception.
